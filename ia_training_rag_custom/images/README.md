According to Alex Karp, only two profiles will have a future in the age of AI:
1️⃣ Specialized trade workers — a butcher or a baker statistically has more job security than a CFO. POs and developers? Awkward silence.
2️⃣ Neurodivergent profiles — harder to define, but the argument holds.

Which leaves me wondering: as a Product Owner, will I soon be competing with an augmented octopus? 🐙

But beyond the joke, there's something more serious worth examining.

Since AI now absorbs pure rationality, the real question becomes: how do we approach problems differently?

Think of a military analogy. Two opposing forces sharing the same thinking tools, the same doctrines, the same mental models — they clash without surprise and without decisive outcome. Decisive action never comes from firepower alone. It comes from divergent intelligence that disrupts the battlefield.

---

But here's where Karp's discourse deserves to be read against the grain.

When a company like Palantir promotes "divergent intelligence," it's not philanthropy. It's the posture of a paranoid dominant player trying to shield itself from anything that could challenge its grip. The real objective: eliminate competitors, not empower individuals.

The true value of divergent thinking isn't where we're told to look.

It lives in the process itself:
→ Divergent thinking generates ideas, explores, questions, disrupts
→ Convergent thinking organizes, structures, decides

One without the other is either chaos or conformity.

AI excels at convergence. The challenge — and the responsibility — is not to surrender divergence.
